Testing Mac Gatekeeper

Testing Notes

Gatekeeper on Mac guards against the execution of any application from unknown developers, here are some short notes when testing application compliance.

The key gotcha to watch out for when testing is to ensure the executable has been quarantined. By that I mean it has the ''com.apple.quarantine'' extended attribute present as Gatekeeper policy is specifically aimed the first time you execute quarantined files.

The easiest way to accomplish this is to email the application to yourself. Applications that download arbitrary content mark them for quarantine automatically, this includes major browsers or email clients.

To verify the quarantine attribute, show file permissions and look for a '@' sign or better yet use the 'xattr' command to specifically what attributes are present on a file.

$ xattr /path/to/file
